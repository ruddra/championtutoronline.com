Teaching Experience

It has been quite varied. Starting in high school, and the summer before college, I taught to groups of 4-7 students GCE 'A' level Economics (both macro and micro). At college, I taught Math from the 4th to 8th grade level to recent immigrant students as a mentor in a community service group. Beyond that, I have acted as a peer tutor in many courses to friends and mentees, from Multivariable Calculus and Partial Differential Equations, to teaching Intro Molecular Biology/ Genetics/ Evolutionary Biology concepts. I took Accounting, Maths (Applied and Pure), Physics, Chemistry, Biology (General and Human) and Economics in my GCE 'O' levels, and continued with Math, Chemistry, Biology, Physics and Economics in my GCE 'A' levels. Note to students and parents: I really enjoy teaching so that my students really understand the material and can eventually solve questions on their own. I find it really rewarding to see them discover their new found independence and confidence in their own abilities. Often it's a simple change from a slight bit of confusion in an earlier, more basic section of the material that leads to struggling in later parts in the course. On the flip side, I do find it disappointing when a student only wants answers without really caring to learn how to solve questions independently.
